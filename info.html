This is me.

Hi.

My name is Martin and I currently am a PhD candidate in the Theoretical Chemical Physics group at the University of Luxembourg. Before joining the TCP group in Luxembourg, I studied (theoretical) chemistry at the Technical University Munich.

My research interests focus around understanding the quantum-mechanical basis of (non-local) interaction mechanisms and many-body effects in complex systems ranging from nano-scale compounds to biomolecular systems. Further, I'm continously working on developing and improving efficient, first-principles based eletronic structure methods for large-scale atomistic modeling. In recent times, this is starting to involve approaches to combine quantum-mechanical principles with efficient machine learning techniques.

Besides research, I enjoy playing football, ice hockey, volleyball, almost any kind of mountaineering – in short, sports. – but also (web) design and music. Also, I'm a passionate cook and retro-gamer.
